Tourist Prediction in Nepal

Nepal, renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, faces significant challenges in effectively allocating resources and maximizing the benefits of its tourism potential. The tourism industry struggles with inefficient resource utilization, resulting in missed opportunities for revenue generation and job creation.

This project focuses on creating a predictive model for tourist arrivals in Nepal using data from 1994 to 2022. The model assists businesses and the government in resource allocation and planning. By understanding tourist trends, stakeholders can make informed decisions to improve the tourism experience, ensuring sustainable growth and efficient utilization of Nepal's landscapes and cultural heritages.

Interest in sports based on gender and the type of picture shown (research design and analysis)

With sports culture being closely associated with masculinity, many gender minorities do not feel welcomed in sporting spaces. Research supports that exercise and sports, especially organized group sports, contribute to better physical and emotional well-being. However, the Women’s Sports Foundation reports that 40% of teen girls are not actively participating in sports, and boys have 1.3 million more sports opportunities than girls. This divide among genders is problematic and demands a solution to create more equitable opportunities for gender minorities.
